Anime & Game

I have selected some currently popular destinations for anime and gaming enthusiasts.

Akihabara

Akihabara, often referred to as "Akiba," is a district in Tokyo, that holds immense appeal for game and anime enthusiasts, particularly among foreign visitors with an interest in Japanese pop culture.

Akihabara is filled with stores offering a vast array of anime-related merchandise, including figurines, posters, clothing, and collectibles. Fans can find items featuring characters from popular anime series, making it a paradise for collectors.

Akihabara is famous for its many electronics retail stores and game centers, and is visited by game enthusiasts and otaku of all ages. Visitors can find a wide range of video games, consoles, accessories, and even rare retro gaming items. It's a haven for gamers looking to explore the latest releases or nostalgic classics.

Universal Studios Japan

Universal Studios Japan (USJ) holds significant appeal for both game and anime enthusiasts. While primarily known for its themed attractions based on popular movies and franchises, USJ also offers experiences that cater to fans of gaming and anime.

As with Universal Studios Hollywood, there is an amusement area themed Super Mario World at Universal Studios Japan, immersing visitors in the iconic world of Mario. Additionally, USJ frequently collaborates with popular anime and gaming franchises to offer limited-time attractions and events. These collaborations may include special themed areas, interactive experiences, or merchandise offerings based on well-known anime or game series.

Ghibli Museum

If you love Ghibli movies, the Ghibli Museum, located in Mitaka, Tokyo, will be a heavenly place for you.

The Ghibli Museum features a variety of interactive exhibits that allow visitors to immerse themselves in the enchanting world of Studio Ghibli. From life-size replicas of film sets to hands-on activities that demonstrate animation techniques, visitors can experience the magic of Studio Ghibli's films in a tangible and engaging way.

In addition, the museum presents films that can only be seen in its museum.

Game and anime-themed cafes

In Japan, there are many "collaboration cafes" based on game or anime concepts. These cafes serve food and beverages based on character motifs, and sometimes there are even places where you can take pictures with the characters. Some of these cafes are only open for a limited time, so if you want to visit one, check carefully to see when it will be open.
